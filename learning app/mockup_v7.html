Course notes

Pre-training = train on massive unlabelled corpus to predict next token. Fine-tuning = continue on smaller labelled dataset. RLHF = human preference feedback → reward model → RL optimisation.

Key: pre-training gives world knowledge, fine-tuning + RLHF align behaviour to human preferences.

My own exploration

RLHF connects to my spec sensitivity work — the reward model is itself a specification choice. Who labels, on what tasks, with what rubric shapes what "aligned" means. Relevant to Paper C.

What's the difference between PPO and DPO?
PPO trains a separate reward model on human preferences, then uses RL to optimise (with KL penalty). Complex but proven.

DPO skips the reward model — trains directly on preference pairs. Simpler and cheaper, similar results.
